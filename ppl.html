People

Here are all the people I have worked with!

Advisors

Dr. Gibney's research interests lie at the intersection of bioinformatics, computational biology, algorithm design, computational complexity, and quantum algorithms. His work explores innovative approaches within these domains, with a focus on advancing theoretical frameworks and practical applications.
Dr. Fox's research explores the intersection of topology and graph theory, investigating how topological structures can facilitate efficient computations on graphs and what graph properties enable such speedups. Dr. Fox has concentrated on the algorithmic foundations of computer science, emphasizing their connections to geometry and topology.
Dr. Satpute's research applied combinatorial optimization techniques to address challenges in natural language processing and wireless communication. Her work focused on algorithmic approaches for multi-document summarization, semantic parsing, and efficient data gathering in edge computing systems, with an emphasis on real-world applications of optimization in computational systems.

Former Advisors

Dr. Hsu's research focuses on advanced materials science, particularly in developing flexible electronics, high-performance solar cells, and innovative photonic curing techniques. Her work explores novel low-temperature processing for metal oxides and transparent electrodes in scalable, sustainable device manufacturing.
Dr. Ricks' research expertise spans wireless networking, network security, and Bayesian networks. He has contributed to the MEWS research group at Carnegie Mellon University: Silicon Valley, focusing on network simulation and security. He inspires first-year students by fostering early engagement and interest in computer science.
Dr. Yapeng Tian leads the Computer Vision and Multimodal Computing (CVMC) Lab. His research tackles fundamental problems in computer vision, computer audition, and machine learning, with applications in multisensory perception, computational photography, AR/VR, accessibility, and healthcare.

Affiliates

Shelly Walters is the Director of Enterprise Data Services at the University of Arkansas. Her work focuses on educational technology, faculty development, and improving online learning through innovative instructional design, professional development initiatives, and fostering communities of practice.
