William T. Doan is an undergraduate researcher with a passion for theoretical computer science, algorithm design, string algorithms, computational biology, ad hoc networks, social networks, and data structures. He is supervised by Dr. Daniel Gibney. Furthermore, Mr. Doan is also working on problems related to the Fréchet Distance, supervised by Dr. Emily Fox.

In addition to his research, Doan is an undergraduate teaching assistant for Data Structures and Introduction to Algorithmic Analysis, taught by Dr. Meghana Satpute. He was also the Lead Teaching Assistant for the laboratories of two courses on Programming Fundaments. His teaching then was advised by Dr. Brian Ricks.

Previously, Mr. Doan did research under Dr. Yapeng Tian. Under Dr. Tian, Doan conducted research in audiovisual novelty detection, audiovisual sound-source segmentation, and object detection according to joint attention at looking targets. Mr. Doan also did work for Dr. Julia W.P. Hsu under a Research Experience for Undergraduates grant he received from the National Science Foundation. He worked on a better grain segmentation model using Meta's SAM as well as FTIR problems.
